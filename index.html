Abstract

WorldComposer is a novel approach for generative high-fidelity simulation with digital cousins for generalizable robot learning and evaluation. This work addresses the challenge of creating realistic simulation environments that can effectively transfer to real-world robot tasks. By leveraging generative models, WorldComposer creates digital twins of real-world environments, enabling robots to learn in simulation with high fidelity to real-world conditions. The system provides a unified framework for generating diverse and realistic environments, allowing for more generalizable robot learning. Experimental results demonstrate that robots trained in WorldComposer simulations show significant improvements in performance when deployed in real-world settings compared to traditional simulation approaches.
